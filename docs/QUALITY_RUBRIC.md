# Chapter Quality Rubric

Score every criterion from 1 to 5.

- **1 — Weak:** misleading, shallow, structurally poor, or incomplete.
- **2 — Developing:** useful material exists, but important gaps remain.
- **3 — Solid:** accurate and practical, with acceptable depth.
- **4 — Strong:** deep, clear, transferable, practical, and well integrated.
- **5 — Reference quality:** unusually strong material worth returning to repeatedly.

## Criteria

| Criterion | Review question |
|---|---|
| Accuracy | Are technical statements correct, current where necessary, and appropriately qualified? |
| First principles | Does the chapter explain why the concept exists and derive it from underlying problems? |
| Transferability | Is the knowledge useful beyond one language, framework, database, or vendor? |
| Practicality | Can the reader apply the ideas to real engineering work? |
| Trade-offs | Are benefits, costs, alternatives, and inappropriate use cases explained? |
| Failure awareness | Are hidden assumptions, naive approaches, and production failure modes explored? |
| Structure | Does the chapter progress logically from motivation to understanding to application? |
| Clarity | Can an experienced mid-level engineer follow it without unnecessary jargon? |
| Depth | Does it go meaningfully beyond introductory definitions? |
| Scope | Does it avoid unrelated expansion and unnecessary repetition? |
| Verification | Do exercises and mastery questions test reasoning rather than memorization? |
| Integration | Does the chapter use consistent terminology and connect to the rest of the book? |

## Approval Threshold

A chapter may be marked **Approved** only when:

- the average score is at least **4.0/5**;
- no criterion scores below **3/5**;
- all material accuracy concerns are resolved;
- no placeholder sections remain;
- required cross-references and references are present;
- the review file records the final scores and remaining non-blocking improvements.

## Required Review Notes

Every review file must include:

1. chapter purpose;
2. prerequisite knowledge;
3. scope boundaries;
4. strongest sections;
5. weaknesses found;
6. technical accuracy concerns;
7. duplication or cross-reference concerns;
8. rubric table with scores and evidence;
9. revision actions completed;
10. final status: `Planning`, `Draft`, `Revision Required`, or `Approved`.
